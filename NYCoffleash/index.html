    Report a Sick or Injured Animal

    🚨
    Is the animal dangerous, vicious, or potentially rabid? Call 911 immediately
    What type of animal?
    🌳 Animals in Parks

    NYC Parks Rangers respond to sick or injured wildlife and strays found in parks. They do not respond to healthy pigeons or squirrels.

    Report via 311 Online Call 311

    ACC accepts drop-offs (not pickups) for injured common wildlife — squirrels, pigeons, robins, small animals. For a licensed rehabilitator, contact the NYS Dept. of Environmental Conservation.

    Call ACC: (212) 788-4000
    🦅 Wild Birds
    🦠
    Bird Flu (H5N1): Do not handle sick or dead waterbirds, raptors, or corvids (crows/ravens). Wear gloves and a mask if handling any sick bird. NYC Bird Alliance: Bird Flu Info ↗ For dead wild birds outside of parks: Report online to NYS Department of Environmental Conservation.

    Wild Bird Fund — NYC's wildlife rehabilitation center for injured and orphaned birds and urban wildlife. Located at 565 Columbus Ave (87th–88th St), Manhattan. Do not feed or give water before transport.

    WBF Rescue Info ↗ 646-306-2862
    🐕 Stray Dogs & Cats

    Contact Animal Care Centers of NYC (ACC). Call before arriving with a stray animal. ACC does not handle healthy strays or nursing mother cats.

    Call ACC: (212) 788-4000 Call 311

    For community cats: CommunityCats@nycacc.org

    🌊 Marine Animals

    Report beached or stranded seals, turtles, or whales via 311. Available 10 AM–6 PM (excluding holidays).

    Call 311 212-639-9675
